CDC H1N1 Flu | Vaccine General Info Content on this page was developed during the 2009-2010 H1N1 pandemic and has not been updated. This  season, vaccines to protect against the 2009 H1N1 virus (sometimes called  “swine flu”) have been produced. Like seasonal flu vaccines, there are two  kinds of 2009 H1N1 vaccines: a "flu shot ";  that is given with a needle, usually in the arm; and a nasal spray flu  vaccine. The same manufacturers that produce seasonal flu vaccines also  produced the vaccines against the pandemic 2009 H1N1 virus in the same way that  the seasonal vaccines are made. Vaccines to protect against 2009 H1N1 are  widely available. CDC is now encouraging everyone to get vaccinated against  2009 H1N1, including people  65 years and older. Learn More…
